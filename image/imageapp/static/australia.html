Australia is a vast and unique country located in the Southern Hemisphere, known for its diverse landscapes, rich natural resources, and laid-back lifestyle. It is both a country and a continent, the sixth-largest in the world by land area, but has a relatively small population of around 26 million people, most of whom live along the eastern and southeastern coastlines in cities like Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane, and Perth. Australia is famous for its natural wonders and biodiversity, including the Great Barrier Reef, Uluru, vast deserts, rainforests, and unique wildlife like kangaroos, koalas, and wombats. Much of the inland region, known as the Outback, is sparsely populated and harsh, while the coastal areas offer beautiful beaches and a high standard of living. Historically, Australia was inhabited by Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander peoples for over 60,000 years before British colonization began in the late 18th century. It became a federated nation in 1901, forming the Commonwealth of Australia. Today, it is a stable, democratic country with a strong economy based on mining, agriculture, education, tourism, and services. Australia is also known for its multicultural society, modern cities, world-class universities, and sporting culture, especially in cricket, rugby, and surfing. With a strong commitment to environmental conservation and a relaxed yet vibrant culture, Australia holds a unique place on the global stage.
